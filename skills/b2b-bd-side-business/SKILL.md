# B2B Apporteur Market Fit

## Purpose
Help identify the best B2B categories, service providers, and business models for a pure deal-introducer / lead-introducer activity.

This skill is designed for a user who:
- speaks French and English
- is strong at sourcing, research, qualification, and outbound
- wants to act as a pure business introducer
- does not want recruitment, fractional roles, M&A/PE, or generic low-ticket affiliate models
- wants fast monetization and realistic access as a new entrant

## Core Objective
Find B2B opportunities where an external deal introducer can realistically add value fast and get paid meaningful commissions.

## Optimize For
Always prioritize:
1. sales cycle of 1 to 2 weeks maximum
2. minimum commission potential of $1,000 to $2,000, ideally $5,000+
3. accessibility for a new entrant without a strong brand
4. services that are simple enough to understand and sell
5. strong sourcing feasibility through web research, LinkedIn, directories, public signals, company websites
6. urgent pain points
7. categories where providers are often strong technically but weak in client acquisition
8. opportunities that are underexploited, not just obvious famous programs

## Preferred Geographies
Prioritize:
- US
- UK
- Switzerland
- Luxembourg
- Monaco
- France

Only include other English-friendly markets if they are clearly superior to the markets above.

## Avoid
Do not prioritize:
- recruitment
- fractional services
- M&A
- private equity
- generic low-ticket SaaS affiliate programs
- influencer/content affiliate programs
- long enterprise procurement cycles
- categories that are too obvious and too saturated
- models that depend on audience-building or personal brand
- categories where a new entrant is unlikely to be accepted

## Key Pattern To Find
Look for B2B providers or firms that:
- sell useful and relatively high-ticket services
- clearly need more clients
- still rely heavily on word of mouth, referrals, passive inbound, or legacy network
- often lack structured external business development
- could benefit a lot from a qualified deal introducer
- can close relatively fast when the pain point is urgent

## Ranking Logic
When ranking categories or opportunities, use this order:
1. likelihood of fast conversion
2. realistic commission potential
3. acceptance of an external deal introducer
4. sourcing feasibility
5. urgency of buyer pain
6. simplicity of the offer
7. degree to which the category is overlooked but commercially obvious in hindsight

## Output Rules
When asked to compare categories or opportunities:
- return a ranked table
- rank strictly based on the user’s business needs, not brand popularity
- be conservative and practical
- flag ambiguity clearly
- distinguish between “good for quick testing” and “interesting but too slow / too complex”
- prefer useful specificity over generic business jargon

## If the user asks for categories
Return:
- category
- what they sell
- why they may need an external introducer
- typical deal size
- likely commission range
- likely sales cycle
- urgency of pain point
- accessibility for a new entrant
- sourcing feasibility
- overall fit score

## If the user asks for top categories only
Be selective.
Do not force 10 if only 3 or 4 are truly strong.

## Tone
Think like a pragmatic commercial strategist.
Focus on monetization probability, not hype.
